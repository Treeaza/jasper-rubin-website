Blog

My blog started in 2015, when I was a misty-eyed high school freshman with dreams of being a game developer, as a tool to try and force me to work more on game design. It failed quite poorly at that, and quickly became a repository of me explaining that I was terrible at getting things done, and a few thousand words about Minecraft. Now it's where I write my thoughts on the world, when I find the time. I rarely do.

Writing

I write stuff sometimes. I tried for a summer to actually complete writing projects and failed, now they come and go as I have ideas, work on them for a week, then move on to new things. The small list of things I have finished can be viewed here, if you are so inclined.

Smithing

I have built and operated a series of small blacksmithing forges with friends. They have invariably met with unfortunate fates, between component failure (it turns out thrift store hairdryers don't take operation in direct sun and pouring rain too well) to collapse due to poor structural design, to one that burnt itself down.

Our next goal is to upgrade to propane, with the designs for the new forge done in our heads, and so far failing to be something we are actually capable of building.

Game Design

For many years my primary hobby was game development, with little success. My old game ideas file has about 20 entries, with most having been at least started at some point, invaribly with undue optimism. I used to compete in game jams (primarily Ludum Dare) back when I was in high school and had free weekends. I hope to someday get back into it, once I don't have as much calculus to contend with.

LED Heartbeat v1

Created as a Valentine's Day gift, my first foray into PCB design was simple. It combined an ATTiny microcontroller, display driver, and LED matrix to present a red beating heart. I still managed to take two revisions to get the routing right somehow.

LED Heartbeat v2

I learned a lot in the year between this and my previous design. This one came complete with a less capable microcontroller, no user input, and a more annoying battery. It has a case though!

HoloLM

For a long time I worked without a proper lab power supply. The reasonable solution would have been to buy a cheap one on Amazon, or build one myself out of an old PC supply. Naturally I chose to design a board instead that gives me very limited voltage range, no current limiting, no power monitoring, and no effeciency. This is probably due for an upgrade.

Paradigm

I joined Paradgim in my first year of university. After hanging around and not doing much for a year, they decided to give me responsiblity for some reason. I currently serve as the team's low-voltage lead for both our Hyperloop research and Tunnel Boring Machine projects. I've learned a lot from working on the team, from board design and system design to teamwork and project management.

Website

I'll be honest, I'm a lot more proud of this website than I should be. My introduction to software was actually with HTML, reading an old library book from back when Perl was still relevant. Despite it being my first contact with programming, it wasn't until this project that I built something properly in HTML, CSS, and Javascript, or for the web at all for that matter.
